# NaijaGenome Manifesto

**Version:** 1.0

**Date:** June 2026

---

# Why We Exist

Africa is home to the greatest human genetic diversity on Earth. Yet despite its scientific importance, African genomic data remains comparatively underrepresented in many computational resources, educational platforms, and software tools used throughout the genomics community.

Researchers studying African genomes often navigate multiple databases, publications, and software platforms to answer a single biological question. Students struggle to learn from fragmented resources. Clinicians and educators face barriers to accessing genomic information in forms that are both understandable and actionable.

NaijaGenome exists to help reduce those barriers.

We believe that knowledge should be easier to discover, easier to understand, and easier to apply.

---

# Our Beginning

NaijaGenome began with a simple question:

> **"As a Nigerian, what can I do with these datasets?"**

The question referred to publicly available Yoruba genome datasets, but it revealed a much larger opportunity.

Rather than analyzing a single dataset in isolation, we envisioned a platform that could help connect genomic data, scientific knowledge, software, and education for the benefit of researchers, students, clinicians, and the broader scientific community.

That question became the foundation of NaijaGenome.

---

# Our Mission

Our mission is to advance African genomics by building open, evidence-based, and accessible software that empowers researchers, educators, clinicians, students, and developers to explore, understand, and apply genomic knowledge.

---

# Our Vision

We envision a future where African genomic data is as accessible, discoverable, and interconnected as any other major genomic resource.

We aspire to build an open platform that supports scientific discovery, education, collaboration, and innovation while helping improve the visibility and accessibility of African genomic research.

---

# What We Believe

## Science should be open.

Whenever legally and ethically possible, scientific software, documentation, workflows, and educational resources should be openly available to the global community.

---

## Knowledge should be accessible.

Genomics should not be understandable only to specialists.

Students, educators, clinicians, researchers, and software developers should all be able to learn from and contribute to genomic knowledge.

---

## Software is scientific infrastructure.

Well-designed software is not merely a convenience; it enables reproducible research, accelerates discovery, and expands access to scientific knowledge.

We believe software is an essential part of modern science.

---

## Education matters as much as research.

Scientific discovery and scientific education strengthen one another.

NaijaGenome will strive not only to provide data and tools but also to help explain the biology, methods, and evidence behind genomic discoveries.

---

## Collaboration creates better science.

No single researcher, institution, or country can fully represent the diversity of African genomics.

Progress depends on collaboration across disciplines, institutions, and nations.

NaijaGenome welcomes contributions from the global scientific community while remaining rooted in the goal of advancing African genomics.

---

## Representation matters.

African populations have made profound contributions to our understanding of human genetics.

Increasing the visibility, accessibility, and responsible use of African genomic resources benefits both African researchers and the global scientific community.

---

# What We Will Build

NaijaGenome is more than a single application.

It is an ecosystem of open tools designed to support African genomics.

This ecosystem may include:

* NaijaGenome Explorer
* NaijaGenome Learn
* NaijaGenome Atlas
* NaijaGenome API
* NaijaGenome AI
* Reproducible bioinformatics pipelines
* Educational resources
* Research tools
* Community contributions

Each component should strengthen the others while remaining useful on its own.

---

# What We Will Not Do

We will not sacrifice scientific integrity for convenience.

We will not present speculation as established evidence.

We will not build features that obscure the source of scientific information.

We will acknowledge uncertainty where it exists.

We will clearly distinguish between evidence, interpretation, and opinion.

We will respect ethical standards, data licensing requirements, and participant privacy.

---

# Our Commitments

We commit to:

* Building reproducible software.
* Documenting our methods.
* Citing our sources.
* Designing accessible tools.
* Supporting open science.
* Encouraging collaboration.
* Improving the visibility of African genomics.
* Learning continuously as the field evolves.

---

# Success

Success is not measured only by downloads, stars, or citations.

Success is achieved when:

* A student learns genomics through NaijaGenome.
* A researcher accelerates discovery using the platform.
* An educator incorporates it into teaching.
* A developer contributes improvements.
* A clinician gains confidence in interpreting genomic information.
* African genomic knowledge becomes easier to access and understand.

---

# Looking Forward

NaijaGenome begins in Nigeria, inspired by publicly available Yoruba genomic datasets.

Its ambition, however, extends beyond any single dataset, institution, or country.

We hope to contribute to a future where African genomics is more accessible, collaborative, and integrated into the broader scientific ecosystem.

Every feature we build, every dataset we integrate, and every line of code we write should move us one step closer to that future.

---

# Our Promise

We build with curiosity.

We build with integrity.

We build for science.

We build for education.

We build for collaboration.

Above all, we build so that African genomic knowledge becomes easier to discover, understand, and apply—for everyone.
